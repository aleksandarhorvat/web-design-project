Music Career

Formation of the Arctic Monkeys (2002—2004)

Turner's parents bought him a guitar for Christmas in 2001. In a 2013 interview, Turner stated that it was during this period that he first began writing songs, using a bass guitar and a large selection of microphones. In mid-2002, Turner, Cook, Nicholson and Helders decided to form a band. The name Arctic Monkeys was coined by Cook. Turner originally did not want to be a singer; a number of students, including Glyn Jones, tried out before Turner became the lead singer. All four were originally beginners on their instruments; they practiced in Turner and Helderson's garage and later in a disused warehouse in Watt. At first, the band's original songs used nonsense words, but Turner gradually began to share his lyrics with his bandmates. They practiced for a year before their first performance. Their first gig was on Friday 13 June 2003 at a local pub called "Grapes". Their repertoire consisted of eight songs, five of which were original.

First albums and commercial success (2005-2007)

In May 2005, they released their first EP, Five Minutes With Arctic Monkeys. The band signed a contract with Domino Records in 2005. Their first album, Whatever People Say I Am, That's What I'm Not, was released in January 2006 and became the fastest-selling debut album in British music history. The album is often regarded as a concept album, relating to UK nightlife. Their second album, Favorite Worst Nightmare, was released in April 2007. They recorded a concert in Manchester, where Richard Holley was a guest.

The Age of the Understatement (2007—2008)

Turner recorded an album with Miles Cain, James Ford and Owen Pallett. They called the band Last Shadow Puppets and the album The Age of the Understatement was released on April 21, 2008. In its first week of sales, it reached number one. By the end of 2008, they had completed a small tour under the auspices of the London Philharmonic Orchestra, starting on August 19 at Portsmouth Guildhall.

Third and fourth albums (2009—2012)

Arctic Monkeys' third album, Humbug, was released in August 2009. The album was produced by Josh Homey, who Arctic Monkeys played in Houston in October 2007. Turner then wrote and performed all six tracks for the soundtrack to Submarine, the first film by Richard Ayoade, friend and director of various Arctic Monkeys music videos. The records were released on March 18, 2011 in the UK and USA. Arctic Monkeys' fourth album, Suck It and See, was released in June 2011.

АМ (2013—2015)

AM was released in September 2013. Turner began writing songs for the band's fifth album (later titled AM) while touring the US with the Black Keys. The album was written and recorded in Los Angeles. On Metacritic, which assigns a normalized score out of 100 reviews by major critics, the album received an average score of 81, based on 34 reviews.

Tranquillity Base Hotel & Casino (2018)

In December 2016, Alec Turner confirmed to the BBC that the band had begun recording their sixth studio album. In May 2017, photos surfaced of the band working on new music in Los Angeles. On April 5, 2018, the band announced the name of their upcoming album, Tranquility Base Hotel & Casino, which was released on May 10, 2018.
