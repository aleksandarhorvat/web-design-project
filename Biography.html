Biography

  1. Turner was born in Sheffield, as the only child of parents Penny and David. His father also grew up in Sheffield, while his mother is from Amersham. Both of his parents worked at the local high school; mother as a German language teacher, and father as a physics teacher, as well as a music teacher. Turner took piano lessons until the age of eight and was exposed to various types of music at home, such as Frank Sinatra, The Carpenters, The Beatles, Led Zeppelin, The Beach Boys, David Bowie and Eagles. His father was a big fan of jazz music and was a member of various big orchestras, where he played saxophone, clarinet and piano.

  2. Turner attended Stockbridge High School (1997-2002). He then spent two years at Barnsley College (2002-2004), where he studied English, Psychology (first year only), Music Technology and Media. After college, Turner's parents reluctantly agreed to let him postpone college for a year to pursue his musical ambitions.

  3. Turner and Matt Helders became friends at the age of seven; they were neighbors and attended the same elementary school. They met Andy Nicholson in high school, and spent much of their teenage years listening to rap artists like Dr. Dre, Wu-Teng Clan, Outkast, Cypress Hill and Roots Manuva. After breaking through the Strokes, Turner was drawn to guitar groups such as the Hives and the White Stripes. Turner attended the band's first gig in 2002, watching the Vines in Manchester. In 2003, aged seventeen, he traveled to London with Helderson and Nicholson to see The Strokes perform at the Alexander Palace, where they met Pete Doherty in the audience.
